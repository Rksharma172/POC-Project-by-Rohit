# HR Leave and Attendance Policy

Document ID: HR-POL-001
Effective date: 2026-01-01
Owner: People Operations
Applies to: Full-time and part-time employees in India and United States offices

## 1. Purpose

This policy explains working hours, attendance expectations, paid leave, unpaid leave, sick leave, holidays, and approval rules. It is intended for chatbot testing only and does not represent a real company policy.

## 2. Standard Working Hours

Employees are expected to work 40 hours per week unless their offer letter or local employment agreement states otherwise.

Core collaboration hours are 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM in the employee's local time zone, Monday through Friday. Teams may define narrower support windows when customer commitments require it.

Employees must record attendance in the time and attendance system by the end of each workday. Missed entries must be corrected within 3 business days.

## 3. Paid Time Off

Full-time employees receive 18 days of paid time off per calendar year. Part-time employees receive prorated paid time off based on scheduled weekly hours.

Paid time off accrues monthly and may be used after the employee completes 30 calendar days of employment. Up to 5 unused paid time off days may be carried into the next calendar year. Any carried days must be used by March 31.

Paid time off requests of 3 or fewer consecutive workdays must be submitted at least 5 business days in advance. Requests of 4 or more consecutive workdays must be submitted at least 15 business days in advance.

## 4. Sick Leave

Employees receive 10 sick leave days per calendar year. Sick leave may be used for the employee's illness, preventive care, mental health needs, or care for an immediate family member.

If sick leave lasts more than 2 consecutive workdays, the employee may be asked to provide a doctor's note or equivalent medical documentation where permitted by law.

Unused sick leave does not convert to cash and does not carry forward unless local law requires otherwise.

## 5. Emergency Leave

Employees may request up to 5 days of emergency leave per incident for bereavement, family medical emergencies, natural disasters, or urgent safety issues.

Emergency leave should be reported to the manager as soon as practical. Formal documentation may be submitted after the employee returns to work.

## 6. Public Holidays

Holiday calendars are published by People Operations by December 15 for the following year. Employees follow the holiday calendar assigned to their primary work location.

If a required business service must operate on a public holiday, the manager must provide either a compensatory day off within 30 days or applicable holiday pay according to local law.

## 7. Unpaid Leave

Unpaid leave may be approved when paid leave balances are exhausted. Unpaid leave longer than 10 consecutive workdays requires approval from the department head and People Operations.

Benefits continuation during unpaid leave depends on local law, benefit plan rules, and leave length.

## 8. Late Arrival and Absence

Employees should notify their manager before the start of the workday if they expect to be late or absent. Three unexplained late arrivals in a rolling 30-day period may result in a documented coaching conversation.

Absence without notice for 3 consecutive scheduled workdays may be treated as job abandonment unless the employee provides a valid reason.

## 9. Approval Authority

Managers may approve paid time off, sick leave, and emergency leave up to 10 consecutive workdays. Department heads must approve leave exceeding 10 consecutive workdays.

People Operations must review any leave request involving medical accommodation, statutory leave, immigration constraints, or a dispute about leave balance.

## 10. Examples

Example 1: An employee with 7 accrued paid time off days requests 4 vacation days for next month. The employee must submit the request at least 15 business days in advance because it is 4 consecutive workdays.

Example 2: An employee is sick on Monday and Tuesday and returns Wednesday. A medical note is not normally required. If the employee remains sick on Wednesday, documentation may be requested.

Example 3: An employee has 4 unused paid time off days on December 31. The 4 days carry into the next year and must be used by March 31.
