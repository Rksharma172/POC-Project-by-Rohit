# Remote Work, Travel, and Expense Policy

Document ID: OPS-POL-003
Effective date: 2026-03-15
Owner: Operations and Finance
Applies to: Employees who work remotely or travel for business

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines remote work expectations, home office equipment rules, business travel approvals, reimbursable expenses, and documentation standards. This is fictional content for chatbot testing.

## 2. Remote Work Eligibility

Employees may work remotely if their role can be performed effectively away from the office, their manager approves the arrangement, and the employee can maintain confidentiality and reliable connectivity.

New employees may be asked to work from an office or approved coworking location during their first 30 calendar days for onboarding.

Remote work from another country for more than 15 calendar days requires approval from People Operations, Legal, Tax, and the employee's department head.

## 3. Remote Work Expectations

Remote employees must be reachable during core collaboration hours and must maintain a workspace suitable for confidential conversations.

Employees must use company-approved communication tools for business messages and meetings. Work documents must not be stored in personal cloud accounts.

Managers may require occasional office attendance for planning, training, customer meetings, or team events with reasonable notice.

## 4. Home Office Equipment

Full-time remote employees may request one external monitor, keyboard, mouse, headset, and laptop stand. Equipment remains company property unless Finance approves a transfer at separation.

Employees may request reimbursement up to USD 300 or local equivalent for ergonomic office equipment once every 3 years.

Internet service reimbursement is capped at USD 50 per month or local equivalent and requires proof of service.

## 5. Business Travel Approval

All overnight business travel must be approved before booking. Domestic travel requires manager approval. International travel requires manager, department head, and Finance approval.

Travel should be booked through the approved travel platform unless an exception is granted by Finance.

Employees should choose reasonable, safe, and cost-effective travel options. The lowest fare is not required when it would create unreasonable travel time, safety risk, or accessibility issues.

## 6. Reimbursable Expenses

The following expenses are generally reimbursable when business-related and properly documented:

- Airfare, rail, bus, taxi, rideshare, and rental cars when approved
- Hotel or serviced apartment lodging
- Meals during business travel
- Visa fees and required travel vaccinations
- Conference registration fees
- Reasonable baggage fees for business travel

## 7. Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following expenses are not reimbursable:

- Personal entertainment
- In-room movies or gaming charges
- Traffic fines or parking tickets
- Airline seat upgrades unless preapproved for medical or accessibility reasons
- Expenses for family members or guests
- Lost personal items

## 8. Meal Limits

Meal reimbursement is limited to USD 75 per day or local equivalent unless a higher limit is approved for a specific city or event.

Alcohol is not reimbursable unless part of a preapproved customer event and itemized according to Finance rules.

## 9. Receipts and Submission Timing

Expense reports must be submitted within 30 calendar days after the expense date. Receipts are required for any expense over USD 25 or local equivalent.

Credit card statements alone are not sufficient when an itemized receipt is available.

Late expense reports may be rejected unless the employee provides a valid reason and manager approval.

## 10. Examples

Example 1: An employee books domestic travel for a one-day client meeting. Manager approval is required before booking.

Example 2: A remote employee buys a chair for USD 420. The reimbursement limit is USD 300 unless a separate accommodation is approved.

Example 3: A meal receipt for USD 27 must be attached because it is over the USD 25 receipt threshold.
