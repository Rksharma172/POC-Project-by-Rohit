# Procurement and Vendor Management Policy

Document ID: FIN-POL-005
Effective date: 2026-05-01
Owner: Procurement and Finance
Applies to: Employees who buy goods or services, approve vendors, or manage supplier relationships

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines purchasing authority, vendor onboarding, competitive bidding, contract review, purchase orders, and invoice approval. It is fictional content for chatbot testing.

## 2. Purchasing Principles

Purchases must serve a valid business purpose, use company funds responsibly, follow approval limits, and avoid conflicts of interest.

Employees must not split a purchase into smaller transactions to avoid approval thresholds.

Any purchase involving personal data, customer data, security tooling, cloud services, or AI services requires additional review before the vendor is used.

## 3. Approval Limits

Managers may approve purchases up to USD 2,500.

Department heads may approve purchases above USD 2,500 and up to USD 25,000.

Finance leadership must approve purchases above USD 25,000 and up to USD 100,000.

The Chief Financial Officer must approve purchases above USD 100,000.

Board approval is required for any single purchase above USD 500,000.

## 4. Competitive Bidding

Purchases above USD 25,000 require at least 3 written quotes unless Procurement approves a sole-source justification.

Sole-source justification may be accepted when only one vendor can meet technical requirements, compatibility requirements, regulatory requirements, or urgent business continuity needs.

Procurement may require a request for proposal for complex services, multi-year agreements, or strategic vendors.

## 5. Vendor Onboarding

New vendors must complete onboarding before work begins or payment is issued.

Onboarding includes business verification, tax documentation, payment details, sanctions screening, conflict-of-interest review, and information security review when applicable.

Vendors handling personal data must complete privacy review and sign required data protection terms.

## 6. Contract Review

Legal must review contracts that include:

- Total value above USD 10,000
- Customer data, employee data, or confidential data
- Auto-renewal longer than 12 months
- Liability caps below the expected risk level
- Exclusivity, non-compete, or unusual indemnity terms
- Use of company logo, trademarks, or public references

Employees must not sign vendor contracts unless they have delegated signing authority.

## 7. Purchase Orders

A purchase order is required before goods or services are ordered when the purchase exceeds USD 5,000.

Invoices without a matching purchase order may be delayed or rejected unless Finance approves an exception.

## 8. Invoice Approval

The business owner must confirm that goods were received or services were performed before approving payment.

Invoices must include vendor name, invoice date, description, amount, tax details, and payment instructions.

Invoice discrepancies must be resolved before payment.

## 9. Conflicts of Interest

Employees must disclose any personal, family, financial, or advisory relationship with a vendor before participating in vendor selection or approval.

Employees must not accept gifts, travel, entertainment, or personal discounts from vendors if the benefit could influence a purchasing decision.

## 10. Examples

Example 1: A team wants to buy software for USD 18,000. Department head approval and Legal review are required because the total value is above USD 10,000.

Example 2: A vendor will process employee addresses. Privacy review and data protection terms are required before onboarding.

Example 3: A manager asks to buy USD 30,000 of services as two USD 15,000 purchases. This is prohibited because purchases must not be split to avoid thresholds.
