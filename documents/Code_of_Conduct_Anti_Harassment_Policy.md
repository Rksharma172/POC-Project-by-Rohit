# Code of Conduct and Anti-Harassment Policy

Document ID: ETHICS-POL-006
Effective date: 2026-01-20
Owner: Ethics and Compliance
Applies to: Employees, contractors, interns, officers, and board members

## 1. Purpose

This policy sets expectations for respectful conduct, anti-harassment, discrimination prevention, conflicts of interest, reporting, investigations, and non-retaliation. It is fictional content for chatbot testing.

## 2. Expected Conduct

Everyone must act professionally, communicate respectfully, follow laws and company policies, protect confidential information, and use company resources responsibly.

Disagreements are expected in normal work. Disagreements must be handled without insults, threats, intimidation, exclusionary behavior, or personal attacks.

## 3. Harassment and Discrimination

Harassment is unwelcome conduct based on a protected characteristic or any conduct that creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment.

Protected characteristics may include race, color, religion, caste, national origin, citizenship, sex, gender identity, gender expression, sexual orientation, age, disability, pregnancy, marital status, veteran status, genetic information, or any status protected by law.

Harassment can occur in person, by chat, email, video meeting, social media, offsite events, or customer and vendor interactions.

## 4. Sexual Harassment

Sexual harassment includes unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, sexually explicit comments, unwanted touching, repeated requests for dates after refusal, or conditioning work benefits on sexual conduct.

Sexual harassment may be committed by any person regardless of gender or reporting relationship.

## 5. Bullying and Abusive Conduct

Bullying includes repeated hostile behavior, humiliation, threats, sabotaging work, spreading malicious rumors, or intentionally isolating someone from work-related communication.

Reasonable performance management, coaching, workload assignment, and business disagreement are not bullying when handled professionally.

## 6. Conflicts of Interest

Employees must disclose situations where personal interests may interfere with company interests. Examples include outside employment with a competitor, supervising a family member, owning a significant interest in a vendor, or accepting gifts that may influence judgment.

Disclosures should be sent to compliance@example.test.

## 7. Reporting Concerns

Concerns may be reported to a manager, People Operations, Legal, Compliance, or the anonymous ethics hotline.

Reports should include what happened, when it happened, who was involved, witnesses, documents, and any immediate safety concerns. Reports may still be made when some details are unknown.

## 8. Investigation Process

The company will review reports promptly, assign a qualified investigator, protect confidentiality to the extent practical, and take appropriate corrective action when policy violations are found.

Employees are expected to cooperate honestly in investigations. Knowingly false reports or knowingly false statements during an investigation may result in discipline.

## 9. Non-Retaliation

Retaliation is prohibited against anyone who reports a concern in good faith, participates in an investigation, refuses to participate in misconduct, or requests a workplace accommodation.

Retaliation includes demotion, threats, reduced hours, exclusion from work opportunities, negative references, or harassment because of protected activity.

## 10. Corrective Action

Policy violations may result in coaching, training, written warning, reassignment, loss of privileges, termination, vendor removal, or legal action.

## 11. Examples

Example 1: A manager repeatedly mocks an employee's accent in team meetings. This may violate the anti-harassment policy.

Example 2: An employee reports suspected harassment and is then excluded from all project meetings. This may be retaliation.

Example 3: A team lead gives direct feedback about missed deadlines using respectful language and documented examples. This is generally performance management, not bullying.
