# Data Privacy and Records Retention Policy

Document ID: LEGAL-POL-004
Effective date: 2026-04-01
Owner: Legal and Privacy Office
Applies to: Employees and contractors who collect, use, store, or delete personal data

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines privacy principles, data subject requests, retention periods, deletion holds, and approval requirements. It is fictional content for chatbot testing.

## 2. Privacy Principles

Personal data must be collected for a specific business purpose, limited to what is necessary, protected against unauthorized access, and deleted when no longer required.

Employees must not collect sensitive personal data unless Legal or the Privacy Office has approved the use case.

Personal data must not be copied into unsupported tools, public AI systems, personal email, or personal cloud storage.

## 3. Personal Data Categories

Basic personal data includes name, work email, job title, business phone number, and office location.

Sensitive personal data includes government IDs, health information, precise location, biometric data, financial account information, union membership, and information about children.

Customer confidential data may include customer contacts, contracts, usage logs, support tickets, and customer-provided files.

## 4. Data Subject Requests

Requests to access, correct, delete, restrict, or export personal data must be sent to privacy@example.test within 1 business day of receipt.

Employees must not respond directly to a formal data subject request unless the Privacy Office instructs them to do so.

The Privacy Office tracks deadlines, verifies identity, coordinates data searches, and approves the final response.

## 5. Records Retention Schedule

Employment records must be retained for 7 years after employment ends unless local law requires a longer period.

Payroll and tax records must be retained for 8 years.

Customer contracts must be retained for 7 years after contract termination.

Security logs must be retained for 18 months unless needed for an investigation.

Routine support tickets must be retained for 3 years after closure.

Marketing consent records must be retained for as long as the consent is active and 3 years after withdrawal.

## 6. Legal Holds

Legal may issue a legal hold when records may be relevant to litigation, investigation, audit, or regulatory inquiry.

Employees must preserve all records covered by a legal hold even if the normal retention period has expired.

Only Legal may release a legal hold. Managers and system owners must not delete held records until Legal confirms release in writing.

## 7. Deletion and Disposal

Electronic records must be deleted using approved system deletion workflows. Physical records containing confidential or restricted information must be shredded or placed in locked disposal bins.

Before deleting a data set, the owner must confirm that the retention period has expired, no legal hold applies, and the data is not needed for a current business process.

## 8. Third-Party Processors

Vendors that process personal data must be reviewed by Procurement, Security, and the Privacy Office before use.

Contracts with processors must include data protection terms, confidentiality obligations, breach notification requirements, and deletion or return obligations.

## 9. Breach Notification

Suspected privacy incidents must be reported immediately to privacy@example.test and security@example.test.

Examples include sending personal data to the wrong recipient, exposing customer files publicly, losing an unencrypted device, or discovering unauthorized access.

## 10. Examples

Example 1: A customer asks support to delete all personal data. The support agent must forward the request to privacy@example.test within 1 business day.

Example 2: A manager wants to delete old payroll records after 5 years. The request must be denied because payroll and tax records require 8 years of retention.

Example 3: Security logs from 20 months ago may normally be deleted unless Legal has issued a legal hold or an investigation requires preservation.
