# Facilities, Health, and Safety Policy

Document ID: FAC-POL-008
Effective date: 2026-02-20
Owner: Facilities and Workplace Safety
Applies to: Employees, contractors, visitors, and vendors at company locations

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines workplace access, visitor management, emergency procedures, incident reporting, ergonomics, office conduct, and safety responsibilities. It is fictional content for chatbot testing.

## 2. Building Access

Employees must use their assigned access badge to enter company offices. Tailgating is prohibited. Employees must not lend badges to another person.

Lost or stolen badges must be reported to Facilities within 1 hour of discovery.

Access badges must be returned when employment or assignment ends.

## 3. Visitor Management

Visitors must be registered before arrival, show identification where required, wear a visitor badge, and remain with a host unless Facilities approves unescorted access.

Visitors may not enter secure areas, server rooms, records rooms, or restricted labs without written approval from the area owner.

## 4. Emergency Procedures

Employees must follow posted evacuation routes during fire alarms, drills, gas leaks, or other emergency instructions.

Do not use elevators during fire evacuations unless emergency personnel instruct otherwise.

Managers should confirm team status after evacuation and report missing persons to emergency coordinators.

## 5. Workplace Injuries and Incidents

Workplace injuries, near misses, unsafe conditions, spills, electrical hazards, and security concerns must be reported to Facilities as soon as possible.

Serious injuries or immediate danger must be reported to local emergency services first, then to Facilities and People Operations.

## 6. Ergonomics

Employees may request an ergonomic assessment through Facilities. Recommended adjustments may include monitor height, chair fit, keyboard position, lighting changes, or alternate input devices.

Medical accommodations related to ergonomics must be coordinated with People Operations.

## 7. Food, Cleanliness, and Shared Spaces

Employees should keep shared spaces clean, label refrigerated food, remove expired items, and report spills promptly.

Strong odors, open flames, and unsafe appliances are not allowed in office kitchens.

Conference rooms should be released when no longer needed.

## 8. Weapons and Violence Prevention

Weapons are prohibited at company locations unless local law requires a different rule and Legal has reviewed the situation.

Threats, intimidation, physical violence, stalking, and intentional property damage are prohibited and must be reported immediately.

## 9. Smoking, Alcohol, and Controlled Substances

Smoking and vaping are allowed only in designated outdoor areas where permitted by law.

Alcohol may be served at company events only with leadership approval and must be handled responsibly.

Illegal drugs and misuse of controlled substances are prohibited at company locations and during company business.

## 10. Examples

Example 1: An employee forgets their badge and asks a coworker to let them in. The coworker should direct the employee to reception instead of allowing tailgating.

Example 2: A visitor needs to enter a server room. Written approval from the area owner is required.

Example 3: An employee sees water near an electrical outlet. The employee should report it to Facilities immediately and avoid the area.
